Churn Rate

Churn rate is the percentage of customers or subscribers who stop using a product or service during a given period. It measures attrition and is a key driver of LTV and unit economics. Lower churn extends customer lifetime and improves the return on acquisition spend.

Formula

Relationship to LTV

Churn rate directly affects LTV. In the simplified LTV formula LTV = (ARPU Ã— Gross Margin) / Churn, higher churn lowers LTV. A business with 2% monthly churn has roughly twice the LTV of one with 4% churn, all else equal. Reducing churn is often a high-leverage way to improve unit economics.

Relationship to ROI

Because churn affects LTV, it affects the return on customer acquisition. Higher churn means shorter customer lifetimes and lower total revenue per customer, reducing the effective ROI of marketing and sales spend. Caveats

Churn can be measured as customer count or revenue. Voluntary churn (canceled) differs from involuntary (payment failure). Cohort analysis is often needed to understand how churn varies by acquisition cohort. Seasonal or one-time events can distort period-over-period comparisons.
